## By Region — Problems and Context

### 🇺🇸 United States
**Buyer profile:** Health food brands, supplement manufacturers, natural grocery distributors, DTC snack brands.

| Problem | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| **FDA Prior Notice** | Must be filed ≥8 hours before sea arrival. Missed or incorrect = refusal of admission. Importer's responsibility but requires precise ETA and product data from exporter. |
| **FSVP (Foreign Supplier Verification Program)** | US importers must maintain documented supplier verification. We provide FSSAI licence, NABL COA, and facility records to support the FSVP file. |
| **Non-NABL COA** | FDA expects FSVP-supporting lab results from accredited labs. Non-NABL COAs do not satisfy this. Common failure from smaller Indian suppliers. |
| **Pesticide MRL** | US EPA MRL list; multi-residue panel must be EPA-compliant, not just FSSAI/EU panel. |
| **Supplier fraud / verification** | No in-country presence; buyers cannot visit easily. First-time buyers especially vulnerable. |
| **HS code reclassification** | Makhana reclassified July 2025 (2008.19.21). Old code causes classification mismatch at CBP. |
| **Heavy metals (supplements)** | Supplement manufacturers require ICP-MS heavy metals panel for botanical ingredients. |

**Content we have:** Makhana import guide, documentation checklist, moringa import guide (FSVP section), price guide.
**Content gap:** US supplement market specifically — DSHEA, identity testing, heavy metals panel for botanical ingredients.

### 🇪🇺 European Union
**Buyer profile:** Organic food importers, supplement manufacturers, food ingredient distributors, private label retailers (Germany, Netherlands, France, Nordics).

| Problem | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| **EU Novel Food (moringa)** | *Moringa oleifera* is authorised; *M. stenopetala* is not. EU importer must hold authorisation before selling. Many buyers discover this after placing an order. |
| **Ethylene oxide (ETO)** | EU limit 0.02 mg/kg. Indian herbal/spice products have been flagged repeatedly in RASFF. Requires explicit ETO residue test on COA. |
| **CHED filing (TRACES NT)** | Must be filed by EU importer's customs broker at port of entry. Triggers physical inspection for higher-risk product categories. |
| **EU Organic 2018/848** | EU organic label claims require certification under EU Regulation 2018/848, not just Indian NPOP. Lot-specific certificates required. |
| **Pesticide MRL (EU — strictest globally)** | Full EU MRL panel required; shorter panels are not accepted. Some India-specific residues exceed EU limits. |
| **Non-NABL COA** | EU border control posts require accredited lab documentation. Non-NABL COAs are rejected. |
| **Species verification (moringa)** | Legal requirement under Novel Food authorisation. COA must confirm *Moringa oleifera*. |
| **Sulphite-free compliance** | EU MRL for SO₂ is ≤50 ppm. Organic buyers require sulphite-free. Requires advance notice and separate production run. |
| **No FTA with India** | Full MFN duty applies. EU GSP for India has changed — verify current status. No preferential route currently. |

**Content we have:** Documentation checklist (CHED section), moringa import guide (EU section), dehydrated buyer guide (sulphite-free, ETO), Lakadong guide (EU organic).
**Content gap:** EU-specific compliance deep dive; EU Novel Food application process for moringa; EU RASFF history for Indian products.

### 🇦🇪 UAE / GCC
**Buyer profile:** Food distributors, hospitality/F&B chains, health food retailers, re-export trading companies.

| Problem | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| **Halal certification** | Mandatory for processed food entering Saudi Arabia and most GCC markets. Must be from SFDA-recognised certifying body. |
| **Certificate of Origin legalisation** | CoO may need MEA attestation + embassy attestation. 3–5 business days; often missed from timelines. |
| **FTA duty not claimed (India–UAE CEPA)** | CEPA has been in force since May 2022. Many buyers still import at MFN rates, missing the preferential benefit. Requires Preferential CoO from FIEO. |
| **Documentation errors** | UAE customs is strict on invoice-packing list consistency. Weight/value mismatches trigger inspection. |
| **Supplier verification** | Large volume of transshipment trade through UAE means product can have obscured origin. Buyers want direct APEDA-registered supplier. |

**Content we have:** FTA blog (UAE CEPA section), documentation checklist (GCC section).
**Content gap:** UAE-specific import process; Halal certification guide; CEPA claiming process step-by-step.

### 🇬🇧 United Kingdom
**Buyer profile:** Health food brands, ethnic grocery distributors, online health retailers, supplement companies.

| Problem | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| **UK retained Novel Food (moringa)** | Post-Brexit, UK has its own framework. EU authorisation does not automatically apply in GB. Northern Ireland follows EU rules under Windsor Framework. |
| **ETO contamination** | UK follows EU-equivalent limits. RASFF alerts apply to UK OPSS border controls. |
| **Non-NABL COA** | UK food safety authorities expect accredited lab documentation. |
| **No UK–India FTA (yet)** | IUFTA negotiations ongoing as of 2025. Full MFN duty applies currently. Once agreed, significant duty reductions expected. Watch for finalisation. |
| **Organic certification** | UK Organic Regulation (retained from EU). Products certified under EU Organic require UK Conformity Assessment post-Brexit. |

**Content we have:** Moringa import guide (UK section), documentation checklist.
**Content gap:** UK-specific post-Brexit import changes; UK Organic requirements; IUFTA update when finalised.

### 🇦🇺 Australia
**Buyer profile:** Natural food brands, supplement manufacturers, organic food distributors, health food retailers.

| Problem | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| **DAFF biosecurity** | Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry requires fumigation certificate (methyl bromide or heat treatment) for many plant-derived products. |
| **BICON import permit** | Certain spices and plant products require import permit. Must be checked before shipment — cannot be resolved after container is loaded. |
| **FTA duty not claimed (India–Australia ECTA)** | ECTA effective December 2022. Many importers still on MFN rates. Requires Preferential CoO. |
| **Pesticide MRL** | APVMA MRL standards; must confirm against AU panel, not just EU/US. |
| **Non-NABL COA** | Australian food standards authority expects accredited lab documentation. |
| **Organic certification** | Australian Certified Organic (ACO) or equivalent required for organic claims. NPOP alone is not sufficient for Australian organic labels. |

**Content we have:** Documentation checklist (Australia section), FTA blog (ECTA section).
**Content gap:** Australian BICON permit guide; Australian organic certification requirements; APVMA MRL specifics.

---

### 🇯🇵 Japan / 🇰🇷 South Korea
**Buyer profile:** Health food ingredient importers, supplement manufacturers, food processors.

| Problem | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| **FTA duty not claimed** | India–Japan CEPA (2011) and India–Korea CEPA (2010) are well-established but many buyers do not claim preferential rates. |
| **Japan MRL standards** | Japan has some of the most restrictive MRL standards globally (positive list system). Multi-residue panel must be tested against Japan's specific list — EU-compliant COA is not sufficient. |
| **ETO contamination** | Japan has flagged Indian herbal products for ETO. |
| **Pesticide MRL (Korea)** | Korea also has strict MRL standards. Korea-specific MRL panel required. |
| **Traceability documentation** | Both markets have detailed traceability expectations. Lot-level documentation linking farm to export is increasingly required. |
| **Heavy metals (Japan supplements)** | Japanese health food regulations for supplements include heavy metals specifications that may be stricter than EU. |

**Content we have:** FTA blog (Japan/Korea CEPA sections).
**Content gap:** Japan-specific import compliance; Japan MRL positive list; Korean food import requirements.
