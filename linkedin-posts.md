## MAKHANA GRADES — 5, 6 & 7 SUTA
*Link: exportdesi.com/blog/makhana-grades-5-6-7-suta-explained*

### Version A — Educational
**Hook:** What does "7 Suta" mean in makhana grading?

Makhana grading is based on bead size — and it directly affects pricing and application.

The higher the Suta number, the larger the bead:

5 Suta = smaller beads (15–18mm) — cost-efficient; ingredient and processing use
6 Suta = medium (18–21mm) — most requested grade for retail snack products
7 Suta = largest (>21mm) — premium positioning; 30–40% price premium over 5 Suta

(Grades 3 and 4 Suta also exist below 5 — used for very low-cost applications or domestic processing.)

Our guide covers:
• How mechanical sieve grading works
• Price variation by grade and why
• Which grade suits which application

### Version B — Comprehensive / Reference
**Hook:** Export documentation from India — the complete checklist.

Every shipment, regardless of destination:
• Commercial Invoice
• Packing List
• Bill of Lading / Airway Bill
• Certificate of Origin
• Phytosanitary Certificate (post-packing; issued by NPPO India)

• Australia: Fumigation certificate + import permit check (BICON)

### Version B — Compliance-Focused
**Hook:** Importing moringa to the EU? Two things to resolve before anything else.

**1. Novel Food authorisation.**
Moringa oleifera leaf is authorised under EU Novel Food regulation. The authorisation is held by the importer — not the Indian exporter. If you don't already hold this or aren't named under an existing authorisation, you need one before you can sell moringa leaf products in the EU.

**2. Ethylene oxide.**
ETO contamination in Indian herbal products is a documented EU RASFF issue. The EU limit is 0.02 mg/kg. Require a NABL COA with an explicit ETO residue result on every EU-bound shipment.

One more: confirm the species. *Moringa oleifera* is authorised. *Moringa stenopetala* is not. These are different plants — species verification on the COA is a legal requirement, not a formality.

## MAKHANA PRICE GUIDE
*Link: exportdesi.com/blog/makhana-export-price-guide*

### Version A — Market Intelligence
**Hook:** Bihar farmgate prices for makhana rose 150% from 2020 to 2025.

₹500/kg in 2020. ₹1,250/kg by 2025.

For international buyers, this isn't a background statistic — it's the context for every quote you receive. The Indian domestic health food market grew dramatically over the same period, competing directly with export buyers for the same Bihar supply.

What moves the FOB price:
→ Bihar monsoon performance (85%+ of global supply from one state)
→ Seasonal timing — post-harvest (Nov–Feb) vs pre-harvest (Jun–Sep)
→ Grade — 7 Suta carries a structural 30–40% premium over 5 Suta
→ Moisture specification (≤6% vs ≤8% standard)
→ INR/USD rate
→ Domestic Indian demand competition

Prices on a Proforma Invoice are valid for 7–14 days. Understanding what drives them is more useful than any static rate table.

### Version B — Buyer Guidance
**Hook:** How to evaluate a makhana price quote — a quick checklist.

Before you treat a quote as a real offer, check that it specifies:

□ Grade (Suta number + mm range — not just "premium export quality")
□ Moisture spec (≤8% standard; tighter if you need it)
□ Purity (≥98%)
□ Packaging format (bulk 25kg vs retail pouch vs custom)
□ Incoterms clearly stated (FOB which port?)
□ HS code matching current classification (2008.19.21 for popped makhana)
□ Validity period (7–14 days is standard)

A quote missing any of these is incomplete. An "indefinitely valid" quote is a red flag — makhana prices move with crop news and currency daily.
